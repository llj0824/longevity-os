Demo Story

The current seed data demos competence. The recommended seed data demos intelligence.

The gap is not that the system lacks modules, commands, or internal agent structure. The gap is that the current seed does not produce a great teaser narrative. It gives us solid reporting, trial status, and biomarker summaries, but it does not naturally create the kind of mystery, explanation, escalation, and resolution that makes a short teaser video feel alive.

Current seed

Can show reliability

We can already show /longevity Weekly report, active trial status, and biomarker review with grounded data.

Main gap

No strong hook

The strongest likely scenes are “here is your summary” scenes, not “here is the surprising thing I figured out” scenes.

Best fix

Seed a better teaser arc

Create a default week with a visible mystery, a coordinator fan-out moment, and a cleaner experiment proposal that resolves the mystery.

Gap Explained

Why the current seed is hard to demo

When people say “the seed data gap,” what they really mean is: what story does the user see in the first 45 to 75 seconds? Right now that story is more “well-instrumented health dashboard” than “agent physician who noticed something, explained it, and proposed a test.”

Gap How it shows up in the teaser Why it weakens the demo
No immediate mystery The report is grounded but often opens with healthy trends and stable progress. Good reporting is credible, but not inherently magnetic in a teaser.
Cross-module insight lands too softly A likely current-week highlight is something like resting-HR trend improvement. That sounds competent, but it does not make viewers feel the coordinator just figured something out.
Multi-agent routing is mostly invisible The coordinator exists, but the current seed does not force a sharp fan-out moment across several departments. The architecture is powerful, but the demo can look like a single smart report bot.
Best experiment scene is caveat-heavy The protein-sleep trial is interesting, but it is more confounded than ideal for a bold teaser claim. The video either overclaims or has to spend precious seconds explaining caveats.
Short version: today’s seed lets us prove the bot works. It does not yet give us the cleanest “watch this bot discover something and act on it” teaser.

Screenplay

Side-by-side teaser scripts

These are not abstract concepts. They are rough screenplay drafts for a short teaser video. The point is to show how the same bot feels different depending on what the seed data gives it to say.

Version A

Teaser we can cut today from the current seed

0:00 - 0:10 Open on Telegram

User: /longevity Weekly report

Bot: “Diet 7/7 days. Exercise 7/7 days. Weight down. Sleep averaged 7.4 hours. Active creatine trial is on track.”

Feels grounded and polished. The weakness is that it opens like a summary video, not a discovery video.

0:10 - 0:22 Show one insight

Bot: “Cross-module insight: resting HR continues to trend down from baseline.”

Accurate, but not especially surprising or cinematic.

0:22 - 0:34 Jump to trial status

User: /longevity How's the creatine trial going?

Bot: “Intervention phase, good compliance, continuing to collect working-memory and reaction-time observations.”

This is useful proof that the product is live, but it is still mostly a status read.

0:34 - 0:48 Finish with biomarkers

User: /longevity My LDL is 105 and hs-CRP is 0.8. How am I doing?

Bot: “Both improved versus baseline. Vitamin D, triglycerides, and inflammation markers all moved in the right direction.”

Still good. The whole teaser now feels like “smart health summary bot.”

Net effect What viewers take away

The bot is real, grounded, and impressive. But the teaser does not make the coordinator or multi-agent reasoning feel uniquely necessary.

Version B

Teaser we could cut after the recommended seed changes

0:00 - 0:10 Open on a problem, not a report

User: /longevity Why did my sleep and HRV crash last week?

Bot: “I found a likely stack of causes: late workouts, lower dinner protein, missed magnesium, and a travel-week schedule shift.”

Now the teaser starts with mystery plus explanation.

0:10 - 0:24 Show the coordinator fan-out

On-screen caption: Diet + exercise + body metrics + supplements.

Bot: “This does not look like a single-cause issue. The pattern is strongest on days with evening lifting and low-protein dinners.”

This is where the Imperial Physician actually looks like a coordinator.

0:24 - 0:40 Escalate into experiment design

User: /longevity What should I test first?

Bot: “I recommend a 3-week evening-workout cutoff trial. I’m having Trial Design draft it and Safety Review check it.”

Now viewers see the internal graph matter, not just exist.

0:40 - 0:56 Show approval with caveats handled

Bot: “Approved with one condition: hold dinner calories steady so we isolate workout timing instead of changing two variables at once.”

This feels like a real physician workflow rather than a generic productivity agent.

0:56 - 1:08 End on live progress

User: /longevity Start it

Cut to later: /longevity How's the trial going?

Bot: “Intervention phase. Sleep onset is already improving, but it is too early to call the effect.”

The teaser now ends with continuity and anticipation.

Net effect What viewers take away

The bot noticed something, explained it across modules, turned it into a disciplined experiment, and kept tracking it. That is a much stronger teaser identity.

Seed Implications

What changes unlock the better teaser

The better teaser does not require a different product. It requires different seed emphasis. We want the default demo week to contain one visible problem, one multi-agent explanation, and one clean experiment handoff.

1. Seed a visible problem week

Add a short travel, stress, or late-workout week that hurts sleep and HRV enough to be noticed immediately.

2. Make the coordinator fan out

Ensure the problem spans diet, exercise, body metrics, and supplements so the multi-agent architecture shows up on screen.

3. Promote one cleaner experiment

Use a flagship trial that can be pitched and approved without a long confounder disclaimer.

4. Put it in the demo week

The strongest insight must appear in the default rehearsal window, not two weeks earlier or later.

Plain-English answer: the seed-data gap is that the current data mostly supports a good report demo, while the recommended data would support a good story demo.
